WHY IT WORKS: THE DRIVER STUDY

Five years of buoy observations joined with ERA5 reanalysis. Knowing the weather during the forecast window (cyan) carries two to four times the signal of anything in the buoy's past (gray): the air-water temperature gap, sustained wind speed (mixing), solar input, and the north-south wind that drives upwelling along this shoreline.

MODEL SELECTION

Six model families ranked by walk-forward cross-validation inside the training years, then scored once on untouched test weeks. Regularized linear regression (lasso) wins at every horizon: with informative lag and forecast-window features, the problem is close to linear and tree ensembles only add variance.

ERROR ANATOMY

Median absolute error stays under 1°F through 24 hours and the distributions are unbiased. The errors concentrate where the water itself moves several degrees in a day, the upwelling and mix-down events, which is exactly the gap the forecast-weather covariates attack.

METHODOLOGY

Data

NDBC station 45174 (Wilmette buoy) standard meteorological records, 2021 to present, hourly means. ERA5 reanalysis supplies historical weather over the site; the GFS/HRRR blend supplies forecast weather at inference. Both via open, keyless APIs; the forecast models are the same NOAA physics behind NWS products.

Models

One lasso regression per horizon: +3, +6, +12, +24 hours and daily means D+1 through D+7. Features are buoy lags and trends, seasonal clocks, and weather aggregated over the forecast window (wind vectors, speed, gusts, air temperature, solar). Alpha chosen by time-series CV inside the training span.

Validation

The final weeks are an untouched test set; model choice happens by walk-forward CV strictly inside the training years. Persistence is reported next to every number. Training uses reanalysis as a stand-in for forecast weather (perfect-prog), so live skill at D+5 to D+7 inherits the weather forecast's own error and will run somewhat worse than the test numbers.

Open data: NOAA NDBC, ERA5 (Copernicus), GFS/HRRR via Open-Meteo. The buoy is seasonal (roughly May to November). Not an official forecast; for safety decisions use NWS Chicago.
